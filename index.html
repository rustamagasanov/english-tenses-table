Simple

Past

[VERB 2]

Completed Action in the Past

I saw a movie yesterday.

A Series of Completed Actions

I finished work, walked to the beach, and found a nice place to swim.

Duration in Past

I lived in Brazil for two years.

Habits in the Past

I studied French when I was a child.

Past Facts or Generalizations

She was shy as a child, but now she is very outgoing.

Present

[VERB 1] + s/es in third person

Facts or Generalizations

Cats like milk.

Repeated Actions

The train leaves every morning at 8 AM.

Scheduled Events in the Near Future

The train leaves tonight at 6 PM.

Now (Non-Continuous Verbs)

He needs help right now.

Future

will + [VERB 1]

"Will" to Express a Promise

I will call you when I arrive.

"Will" to Express a Voluntary Action

I will send you the information when I get it.

"Be going to" to Express a Plan

He is going to spend his vacation in Hawaii.

"Will" or "Be Going to" to Express a Prediction

John Smith will be the next President.

John Smith is going to be the next President.

Progressive

Past

was/were + [VERB 4]

Interrupted Action in the Past

I was watching TV when she called.

While we were having the picnic, it started to rain.

Specific Time as an Interruption

Last night at 6 PM, I was eating dinner.

Parallel Actions

I was studying while he was making dinner.

Atmosphere

When I walked into the office, several people were busily typing, some were talking on the phones, and customers were waiting to be helped

Repetition and Irritation with "Always"

She was always coming to class late.

Present

am/is/are + [VERB 4]

Now

They are reading their books.

Longer Actions in Progress Now

I am studying to become a doctor.

Near Future

I am meeting some friends after work.

Repetition and Irritation with "Always"

She is always coming to class late.

He is constantly(always) talking. I wish he would shut up.

Future

will be + [VERB 4]

Interrupted Action in the Future

I will be watching TV when she arrives tonight.

Specific Time as an Interruption in the Future

Tonight at 6 PM, I am going to be eating dinner.

Parallel Actions in the Future

Tonight, they will be eating dinner, discussing their plans, and having a good time.

Atmosphere in the Future

When I arrive at the party, everybody is going to be celebrating. Some will be dancing. Others are going to be talking.

Perfect

Past

had + [VERB 3]

Completed Action Before Something in the Past

I had never seen such a beautiful beach before I went to Kauai.

Duration Before Something in the Past (Non-Continuous Verbs)

We had had that car for ten years before it broke down.

Present

have/has + [VERB 3]

Unspecified Time Before Now

I have seen that movie twenty times.

Duration From the Past Until Now (Non-Continuous Verbs)

Mary has loved chocolate since she was a little girl.

Future

will have + [VERB 3]

Completed Action Before Something in the Future

By next November, I will have received my promotion.

Duration Before Something in the Future (Non-Continuous Verbs)

I will have been in London for six months by the time I leave.

Perfect Continuous

Past

had been + [VERB 4]

Duration Before Something in the Past

They had been talking for over an hour before Tony arrived.

Cause of Something in the Past

Sam gained weight because he had been overeating.

Present

have/has been + [VERB 4]

Duration from the Past Until Now

She has been working at that company for three years.

Recently, Lately

Recently, I have been feeling really tired.

She has been watching too much television lately.

Future

will have been + [VERB 4]

Duration Before Something in the Future

They will have been talking for over an hour by the time Thomas arrives.

Cause of Something in the Future

Jason will be tired when he gets home because he will have been jogging for over an hour.
